The switchless lamp

Ljus


Ljus is a switchless interactive lamp driven by the user’s hands movements. Thanks to two Time of Flight (ToF) sensors placed one in front of the other, the system detects if the user placed their hands in between and it reacts accordingly.

The system is able to detect one-hand or two-hands interactions. One-hand interactions serve to extend or push the beam of the light whereas two-hands interactions are used to adjust the size of the beam depending on the user’s preferences. The lamp also turns on/off as soon as the sensors detect some interaction with the system.

In order to calibrate the sensors and code the system, different panels with different reflection values (brightness) were used. The light was an RGB LED strip in which the number of LEDs and their dimming were also programable setting.

The final prototype consisted of two 3D-printed stands were the sensors and the Arduino were placed, the LED strip and a light diffuser on top of the LED strip.
