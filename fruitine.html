An Interactive Fruit Bowl

The Fruitine

By the time this project is developed in Sweden, statistics show that only 20% of Swedish population eat the minimum daily fruit recommendation. The goal is to design an interactive physical artifact that nudges, incentivizes or encourages very specific lifestyle changes, such as achieving a healthy life by eating the recommended servings of fruit a day – between two to five. Based on that premise and on some others project checkpoints, the Fruitine has been created.

Fruitine is the project resulting from the course Physical Interaction design and Realization of the Interactive Media Technology Master's at KTH and it consists of two standalone artifacts: the ‘external reminder’ (left side of the picture) and the ‘fruit bowl’ (right side of the picture):

From the point of view of the interaction with the user, the decision of having a non-intrusive interaction is taken. There are several studies that state that humans prefer ordered, symmetrical patterns over random patterns. As a result of these studies, the individuals shown less distraction if the stimuli surrounding them were symmetric rather than “broken” (as we decided to call it). Thus, the user would be notified that he/she needed to eat fruit with a shuttle warning; a drawn pattern being broken on both the bowl and the reminder surfaces.

The bowl incorporates four load sensors at the bottom which are constantly measuring the weight of the bowl. Depending on the weight changes, three different states can be differentiated: the user has taken a piece of fruit, the user has not taken any piece of fruit (within a programmable amount of time), and the fruit bowl is empty. Different actions could therefore happen depending on that states which are basically breaking the pattern of both surfaces (bowl and reminder) as a warning signal and/or lighting up the LED lights on the external reminder. Regarding the technical implementation, the communication between the bowl and the remainder is over WiFi and the prototyping platforms to control the sensors and actuators are built on Arduino.

Additional material for this project is the Instructable's page for further details on the building process and the GitHub repository where the code of the Arduinos prototyping platforms is included.

Project members: Magnus Lundh Haaland, Judith Sirera I Pulido, Rebecka Möller, and Elena González Rabal.
