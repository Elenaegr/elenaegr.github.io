Interactive sustainable technology

The LinGen


The LinGen is a human-powered batteryless device resulting from my master’s thesis of the program Interactive Media Technology. The aim of human-powered devices is to remove their batteries and to power them by using the energy coming from the human interactions with it.

The LinGen generates energy thanks to the electromagnetic induction working principle. Coils and magnets are the main ingredients. A set of coils and a rod with magnets inside are the main tools to generate energy in the LinGen system. When the user slides the rod through the set of coils, an alternating current occurs as a result. However, many of the daily life electronics need a direct current for which a rectifying circuit has been implemented.

A final application was developed to test the system. The LinGen was connected to an antenna which sent encoded information such as the tilt of the rod, the speed at which the user is sliding the rod, and the direction of the movement (forward of backwards). This information was sent to a receiver connected to the computer and then decoded. The decoded information was mapped to a music program (Ableton Live) that interpreted the users’ movements and acted accordingly by reproducing some sounds and displaying a visual animation.

The huge milestone of the thesis was to be able to power the sender with no batteries whatsoever. All the energy needed was coming merely from the user’s interaction with the system.

If you are interested in knowing more details about the thesis project, follow this link to download the paper: "Exploring electromagnetic induction as a power source for interactive human-powered devices".

The project was suppervised by Anders Lundström who was at the time a KTH researcher and he is currently an associated professor at Umeå University. Anders is undergoing exciting researches on the field that you can follow on his KTH profile page.
